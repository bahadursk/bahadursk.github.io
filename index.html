The Research Group

AURA Lab — Advanced Unimolecular Radiative Afterglow

Bridging Molecular Mechanisms with Optoelectronic Performance

Our scientific curiosity begins with exploring basic excited-state processes to explain persistent, long-lived luminescence in synthetic organic platforms. Under my supervision, the AURA Lab translates those fundamental spectroscopic concepts into lightweight, non-toxic organic emitters optimized for high-contrast bioimaging, advanced optical storage, displays, and security applications.

Explore our Research Lines ›

01

Organic Afterglow & Phosphorescence

Developing high-resolution persistent long-wavelength emitters and anti-counterfeiting platforms through mechanism-driven chemical design.

02

Excited-State Spectroscopy

Analyzing radiative pathways, donor-acceptor interactions, spin-flipping mechanisms, and time-resolved decay characteristics.

03

OLED & TADF Technologies

Optimizing external quantum efficiency (EQE) and limiting roll-offs in Thermally Activated Delayed Fluorescence devices.

04

Functional Materials

Designing flexible, environmentally non-toxic polymers and materials for robust lighting, sensing, and security readout.
