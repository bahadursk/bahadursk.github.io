AURA Lab
Advanced Unimolecular Radiative Afterglow

Fundamental and applied research at the interface of photophysics, spectroscopy, and organic materials, focusing on long-lived emission, excited-state dynamics, and next-generation photonic materials.

Explore Our Research

Research Focus

Long-Lived Organic Emitters

Persistent & Delayed Emission Materials

Development of organic molecular and polymeric systems exhibiting thermally activated delayed fluorescence (TADF), room-temperature phosphorescence (RTP), and ultralong afterglow.

Excited-State Photophysics

Charge Transfer & Energy Relaxation

Understanding excited-state decay pathways using steady-state and time-resolved spectroscopy to unravel structure–property relationships.

Organic & Hybrid Materials

Functional Molecular Design

Rational design of donor–acceptor systems for optoelectronic and photonic applications including OLEDs and sensing platforms.

Principal Investigator

Dr. Bahadur Sk

C. V. Raman Postdoctoral Fellow at the Materials Research Centre (MRC), Indian Institute of Science (IISc), Bangalore.

My research interests lie in the development of organic emissive materials and understanding their radiative and non-radiative decay processes across different time scales.

The AURA Lab aims to develop energy-efficient, lightweight, non-toxic organic photonic materials for applications in lighting, displays, sensing, and security technologies.
